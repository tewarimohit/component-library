1) The Dawn of a Revolution

September 5, 2021

Technology is something that makes our lives hassle free, entertaining, surprising, memorable and full of possibilities. This word possibility in itself is the key to a whole new universe of technologies, it's a kind of loop you can say.

We are certain to this fact that we do not get technology in a day, it takes countless revolutionary intellectual minds working day in and day out in an organised way with their cumulative organised efforts. As a result, in this modern world computers set a perfect example to showcase what humankind is capable of.

It all began when i was a kid, fortnate enough of having early access to computer, for me the whole purpose of this Charles Babbage's refined complex but simple looking machine was to play games.

For the world a computer works in 3 simple steps - Firstly it takes input from the user in the form of raw data, secondly it processes that data and thirdly gives the user output in the form of information but for me.. na-aa...

The 3 simple steps were - Firstly press UPS and CPU to open it, secondly choose the installed game icon on the screen and thirdly double click the icon and play.

In school there was a temple called Computer Lab where every class used to enter in a line after putting their shoes out to to enter and get the glimpse of the divine computer as if this was the place to attain ''Nirvana - the ultimate state of bliss''.

At times two or three kids sharing the same screen with their bright faces and sparkling eyes used to start the computer as per the SOP instructed by the teachers.

The pressure at the moment used to be intense as that of launching a satellite by the Space Research Organisations like one mistake of pressing wrong button and every other finger will point towards you.

In that holy place i have seen bliss turning into misery when noises like that of crowd surrounding the prime seller of a fish market, slight or intense odour of socks could be heard or sensed respectively due to unexpected power cut. But altogether it was fun during school days.

Now when I look back at that kid playing games on that computer and compare it to today's self aspiring to create and develop websites, from working in a school's computer lab to work in the giant institutions of IT world. I realise how the technology is capable of igniting minds and revolutionizing the Whole World.
